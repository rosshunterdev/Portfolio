About Me

Bridging Healthcare Operations & AI Technology

I'm Ross Crawford, an AI implementation specialist with a unique background combining healthcare coordination, psychology, and hands-on technical development. I help healthcare organizations implement AI solutions that their teams will actually adopt and use.

For 3+ years, I worked as a support coordinator at NZCL in New Zealand, managing NDIS-equivalent support for clients with complex needs. I conducted comprehensive assessments, coordinated multiple service providers, navigated intricate CRM systems, and managed stakeholder communication across healthcare providers, government agencies, and families. I maintained 100% documentation accuracy while juggling competing priorities in high-stakes environments.

Before that, I worked at Emerge Aotearoa's crisis respite facility with suicidal teenagers, providing de-escalation, crisis intervention, and relationship building during acute situations. These experiences taught me how to build trust with hesitant or disengaged users, navigate complex systems under pressure, and understand the human factors that determine whether people will adopt new processes or tools.

My Bachelor's degree in Psychology gives me insight into user behavior, motivation, and resistance to change—critical skills for successful technology implementation. I understand why healthcare professionals resist new systems, how to identify adoption barriers, and how to design implementation strategies that account for cognitive load and workflow disruption.

On the technical side, I'm building AI tools using OpenAI's API and RAG systems, and I have hands-on development experience with Python, C#, JavaScript, and modern development workflows. I understand both how to build these systems and how to explain them to non-technical stakeholders.

What makes me different is that I've lived on both sides: I've been the end user struggling with poorly implemented systems, and I've built the technical solutions. I know what questions healthcare professionals ask, what concerns they have, and what they need to feel confident adopting new technology.

I'm relocating to Melbourne in March 2026 and seeking AI implementation, customer success, or product operations roles in healthcare technology—particularly with companies like Governa.ai building AI solutions for aged care and disability services.
