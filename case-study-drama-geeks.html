Client case study

Drama Geeks

Performing arts school, South Shields UK

Full redesign and rebuild — from a tired multi-page site to a warm, trust-building presence that converts parents into bookings.

View live site
Role Designer & Developer
Stack HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Netlify
Year 2025

The brief

Charlotte runs Drama Geeks, a performing arts school for children in South Shields. The existing site was functional but wrong in feel — corporate in tone where the school is warm and community-oriented, and scattered in structure where parents needed clarity. Finding class schedules, fees, or how to actually book a place required too much effort.

The brief was straightforward in principle and demanding in execution: make parents feel welcome the moment they land, and give them a clear, obvious path to enrolment. The school's reputation is built on personal connection; the site needed to reflect that.

Mobile experience was non-negotiable. Most parents in this demographic browse on phones — often quickly, while distracted. Every decision had to work at 375px before it worked anywhere else.

My approach

Brand and tone

The redesign led with warmth — a diagonal hero composition, soft cream backgrounds, and a Fraunces/Poppins type pairing that balances character with legibility. The colour palette (cream, near-black, warm blue, and accent yellow) was drawn from the school's visual identity and pushed toward something that reads friendly rather than institutional.

Information architecture

I restructured the site around the three questions parents actually ask first: can my child try a class, what does it cost, and when and where does it happen. Class pages, a clear fees breakdown, and a show listings section each got their own space rather than being buried in walls of text.

Conversion flow

The booking and contact paths were made frictionless — clear calls to action on every page, a contact form that does not ask for more than necessary, and a booking flow that points parents to the right place without making them hunt.

Mobile-first build

The site was built mobile-first throughout. Navigation collapses cleanly, images scale appropriately, and the most important information — class times, fees, contact — surfaces at the top of the flow on small screens.

Selected screens

Outcome

The site went live in 2025 and replaced the old presence entirely. It is now the primary public face of Drama Geeks — the first thing parents see when they search for the school, and the place Charlotte points prospective families when they get in touch.
